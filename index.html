Research (Research Statement)

My research explores critical questions at the boundaries of data-driven learning: How can models efficiently handle infinite streams of data? How can models sustain effective learning in limited labeled-data regimes? These challenges drive my development of practical solutions for efficient, adaptive data processing in AI systems. I am also interested in uncovering novel insights from data, particularly in scientific domains.

  • Memory-efficient inference and training through data/feature compression (e.g., key-value in Transformers) [KVzip, Context Memory, Dataset Condensation].
  • Synthetic data generation to enhance model training with limited human-labeled data [Puzzle Mix, Co-Mixup].
  • Building relational structures within data to characterize and clean data [Neural Relation Graph].
  • Identifying causal relationships among human genes with data-driven learning [Targeted Cause Discovery].
